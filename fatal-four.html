The four leading causes of road deaths

Focusing on these risks can reduce harm for everyone on the road.

  1. Speeding: higher speeds increase crash severity and reduce reaction time.
  2. Distraction: mobile phones, navigation systems, and passengers all take attention away from driving.
  3. Impaired driving: alcohol, drugs and fatigue impair judgement and control.
  4. Failing to wear seat belts: not wearing a seat belt in the event of a crash significantly increases the risk of serious injury or death.

These are the risks that need to be addressed first, every time you travel.

Speed awareness

Stick to the limit and adjust for weather, traffic and road type. A few mph can save lives.

Remove distractions

Set your route before you start driving and use hands-free only when it is safe and legal.

Reduce the risks

  • Take regular breaks on long journeys to avoid fatigue.
  • Plan your journey and leave extra time so you don’t rush.
  • Check mirrors frequently and watch for vulnerable road users.

If you encounter dangerous driving, keep a safe distance and report it to the police or local authorities when it is safe to do so.

These four dangers are preventable. Focus on them and help protect everyone on the road.
