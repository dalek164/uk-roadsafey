Getting started behind the wheel

Learning to drive safely is about more than passing a test — it’s about building responsible habits.

  • Theory first: learn the Highway Code and hazard perception before practical lessons.
  • Choose a good instructor: a qualified, patient instructor helps develop safe habits.
  • Practice regularly: consistent lessons and practice help you stay calm in traffic.

Focus on the simple routines that make progress more consistent and safer.

Understand hazards

Scan the road ahead, anticipate actions, and learn to use mirrors effectively.

Respect the process

Take your time. Don’t rush the exam and learn to drive comfortably in different weather conditions.

Advice for learner drivers

  1. Keep calm and focus on steady progress.
  2. Ask questions and review mistakes with your instructor.
  3. Read about the test routes and practice common manoeuvres.

Learning to drive is a journey. Start it safely and keep improving every mile.
