Be a good egg on the road

Good road safety is about respect, attention and preparation.

  • Be visible: use lights, signal clearly, and wear bright clothing if walking or cycling.
  • Be courteous: let others merge, give way when safe, and avoid aggressive driving.
  • Be prepared: check your vehicle and carry emergency kits before longer journeys.

Smart habits are the easiest way to make your travel safer and more confident.

Plan ahead

Review your route, check weather and traffic, and make sure you have enough time to travel safely.

Respect other users

Share the road with cyclists, pedestrians and horse riders. Slow down and keep a safe gap.

Every journey counts

Good habits every time keep the entire community safer.

  • Check mirrors at every opportunity.
  • Never assume another road user has seen you.
  • Stick to the rules and set a positive example.

Choose safe decisions and help make Britain’s roads safer for everyone.
